### 2.2 New Jersey SREC-II (Successor Solar Incentive)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Program** | Successor Solar Incentive (SuSI) — Administratively Determined Incentive (ADI) track |
| **Administered by** | NJ Clean Energy Program (under NJ Board of Public Utilities) |
| **Basis** | Production-based: one SREC per 1,000 kWh (1 MWh) generated |
| **ADI Rate (2025-26)** | **~$85/MWh** ($0.085/kWh) — locked for 15 years from registration date |
| **Duration** | 15 years from the date of interconnection/registration |
| **Applies to** | All residential net-metered solar systems in NJ |
| **How it works** | Every MWh your system produces earns one SREC worth ~$85. Payments are guaranteed for 15 years. |

**Example annual SREC-II earnings:**

| System Size | Estimated Annual Production | Annual SREC-II Earnings |
|-------------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| 6 kW | ~8,100 kWh (~8.1 MWh) | ~$689/year |
| 8 kW | ~10,800 kWh (~10.8 MWh) | ~$918/year |
| 10 kW | ~13,500 kWh (~13.5 MWh) | ~$1,148/year |
| 12 kW | ~16,200 kWh (~16.2 MWh) | ~$1,377/year |

> **15-year total value:** A typical 8 kW system could earn $918 × 15 = **~$13,770** in SREC-II payments over the life of the program.

**How to register:**
- Register through the NJ Clean Energy Program's GATS (Generation Attribute Tracking System)
- Registration must happen within 6 months of interconnection
- SREC payments are typically processed quarterly
- SRECs can also be sold on the spot market for potentially higher prices (current market price ~$180/SREC for forward years)

**Important SREC-II Notes:**
- The ADI rate is administratively set and does not fluctuate with market conditions
- Rate is locked for the Energy Year (June through May); current rate is $85/MWh for Energy Year 2025-26
- If a utility fails to meet solar procurement targets, they must buy SRECs at the SACP (Solar Alternative Compliance Payment) of $218 — this puts a floor on SREC value
- SREC registration locks in the rate for 15 years — register as soon as possible after interconnection
- The program has been renewed multiple times; legislative risk exists but is considered low given NJ's clean energy goals

### 2.3 New Jersey Net Metering (1:1 Full Retail)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Program** | NJ Net Metering (N.J.S.A. 48:3-82) |
| **Credit rate** | **Full retail rate** — 1 kWh exported = 1 kWh credited at your utility's retail price |
| **True-up** | Annual; any remaining credit is forfeited at the annual true-up date |
| **Max system size** | 50 kW for residential (well above any residential system) |
| **Impact** | One of the strongest net metering policies in the nation — much more favorable than California's NEM 3.0 |

**How NJ net metering works:**
- Every kWh your solar produces that you don't use is exported to the grid at full retail rate
- This is a 1:1 credit — you get back the full retail rate you would have paid
- Your meter "runs backward" as you export; you only pay for net usage each month
- At annual true-up, any remaining credits are forfeited (no cash payout)
- **No battery required** — unlike CA's NEM 3.0, NJ's net metering makes solar valuable even without batteries

**PSE&G Retail Rate Context (June 2026):**
- Average all-in rate: ~$0.27/kWh
- Net metering credit: ~$0.27/kWh for every kWh exported
- This is significantly more valuable than CA's NEM 3.0 avoided export rates of $0.05-0.12/kWh

#### Time-of-Use Rate (RS-TOU-3P) — Rolling Out June 2026

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Program** | PSE&G Time-of-Use-Dual-Prime (RS-TOU-3P) |
| **Effective date** | **June 1, 2026** — mandatory rollout for all PSE&G residential customers |
| **Winter off-peak** | ~$0.105/kWh (October 1 – May 31) |
| **Winter mid-peak** | ~$0.265/kWh |

| **Summer rates** | Similar structure with higher on-peak rates during summer months |

**Why TOU matters for solar + battery:**
- Battery can charge during off-peak hours (~$0.105/kWh) and discharge during on-peak (~$0.385/kWh)
- TOU arbitrage savings: ~$0.28/kWh per full cycle
- Solar production during peak hours (afternoon) provides direct offset at the highest rate
- **Recommendation:** Enroll in TOU-D-PRIME for maximum solar + battery value

> **Note:** PSE&G is rolling out TOU rates to all residential customers starting June 1, 2026. The homeowner can try the TOU rate risk-free for 12 months and switch back to the standard rate if preferred.

- Level 2 charger rebate: up to $500

### 3.3 Battery + Solar Synergy Under Net Metering + TOU

New Jersey's combination of full retail net metering and the new TOU rate structure creates a compelling value case for solar + battery:

1. **Solar self-consumption:** Solar production during daytime offsets usage at full retail rate (~$0.27/kWh)
2. **Net metering credits:** Excess solar exported earns 1:1 credit at full retail rate
3. **TOU arbitrage (with battery):** Charge battery during off-peak (~$0.105/kWh), discharge during on-peak (~$0.385/kWh)
4. **SREC-II income:** Earn ~$85/MWh for all production, guaranteed for 15 years

**Estimated battery value under TOU:**
- Battery charges at night (off-peak ~$0.105/kWh)
- Discharges during peak hours (4-9 PM, ~$0.385/kWh)
- Savings per full cycle: ~$0.28/kWh × usable capacity × 90% efficiency
- Realistic annual cycling savings: ~$1,500-3,000/year (150-200 full cycles, accounting for seasonal variation)
- Plus: self-consumption of solar during peak hours adds additional value

## 5. Requirements & Considerations

### 5.1 Permitting Timeline

**New Jersey's Smart Solar Permitting Law (S4100/A5264):**
- Signed by Governor Murphy in December 2025
- Requires a statewide automated permitting platform for residential solar
- Aims to provide "instant permits" and reduce soft costs ($3,800-4,500 currently added by permitting)
- Implementation under the new administration of Gov.-elect Sherrill
- As of early 2026, the platform is being developed; current timelines reflect pre-smart-permitting conditions

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Design & engineering | 1-2 weeks |
| Local building permit | 2-6 weeks (NJ has some of the slowest permitting in the country; Smart Solar law aims to fix this) |
| PSE&G interconnection application | 2-4 weeks |
| Installation | 1-2 days (panels only); 2-3 days (panels + battery) |
| Inspection (municipal) | 1-3 weeks after installation |
| PSE&G Permission to Operate (PTO) | 2-4 weeks after inspection |
| **Total timeline (normal conditions)** | **3-6 months** |

> **Note:** NJ has historically had the third-slowest permitting timelines for residential solar in the country. The Smart Solar Permitting Law should significantly improve this, but implementation is ongoing.

### 5.4 Resilience Benefits

- PSE&G outages occur during storms, heat waves, and winter weather events
- Battery system can power essential loads for 1-3 days (depending on size and load selection)
- Backup power value: difficult to quantify but significant in NJ's storm-prone coastal/suburban environment
- NJ's humid summers mean AC is often essential during outages — battery can keep HVAC running

### 5.5 Safety and Code Compliance

**NJ-Specific Requirements:**

- **NEC 2023:** New Jersey has adopted the 2023 National Electrical Code, which includes updated requirements for energy storage systems (Article 706)
- **Rapid shutdown:** Required for all new PV installations per NEC 2023
- **Arc-fault protection:** Required for all new PV installations per NEC 2023
- **Battery fire code:** NJ Fire Code (based on International Fire Code) has requirements for battery energy storage systems, including location, fire protection, and ventilation for lithium-ion systems
- **Seismic requirements:** Less critical in NJ than CA, but equipment must meet standard mounting requirements
- **Wind load calculations:** NJ has coastal wind exposure; panel mounting must be engineered for local wind loads
- **Emergency disconnects:** NEC requires clearly labeled emergency disconnects for both PV and battery systems
- **Roof penetrations and warranty:** Panel mounting penetrations can void existing roof warranties; coordinate with roof replacement if needed
- **Permitting:** All work requires municipal building permits, electrical permits, and PSE&G interconnection approval
- **Inspections:** Municipal inspection before PSE&G Permission to Operate (PTO)

**Questions to ask your installer:**
- Does your design include NEC 2023 rapid shutdown compliance?
- Is the equipment wind-load rated for NJ coastal/suburban conditions?
- How do you handle NJ Fire Code requirements for battery installations?
- Will you coordinate with the local municipal inspector for permits?
- What emergency disconnects are included in the design?
- How do you protect existing roof warranties?

## 9. Key Assumptions & Caveats

1. **Federal ITC is gone for owned systems** — The 30% federal tax credit was repealed effective January 1, 2026. This is the single biggest change from pre-2026 NJ solar economics. PPA/lease systems may still benefit through commercial Section 48E.

2. **SREC-II rate is locked but program risk exists** — The $85/MWh ADI rate is locked for 15 years from registration, but the underlying program could face legislative changes. NJ has consistently renewed solar incentive programs, so this risk is considered low.

3. **Roof capacity is estimated** — Actual capacity requires professional assessment; typical West Caldwell homes can accommodate 8-14 kW.

4. **Cost estimates are ranges** — Actual costs vary by installer, equipment, and site conditions. NJ benchmark $2.50-3.20/W.

5. **Payback calculations** — The payback periods shown assume current rates. NJ electricity rates have historically risen 4-6%/year, which shortens actual payback. The 6-9 year payback cited in some sources assumes the federal ITC is still available and is no longer accurate.

6. **Net metering rules could change** — Future legislation may alter net metering policies, though NJ's full retail net metering is well-established and politically popular.

7. **Battery degradation** — Tesla Powerwall 3 is rated for 100% capacity retention for 10 years. Real-world degradation is likely 1-2% per year. A 13.5 kWh system may be ~12-13 kWh after 15 years.

8. **Four-season climate** — NJ has significant seasonal variation. Summer production is strong; winter production is reduced by cloud cover and shorter days. Annual average is ~4.5 peak sun hours.

9. **TOU rate rollout** — PSE&G is rolling out TOU rates starting June 1, 2026. The homeowner can try the TOU rate risk-free for 12 months. TOU improves battery economics through peak shaving.

10. **GSESP Phase 2 timing** — Residential battery incentives are expected in late 2026 but dates are not confirmed. This is a key variable for battery ROI calculations.

11. **Smart Solar Permitting implementation** — The new automated permitting platform is being developed. Current timelines reflect pre-smart-permitting conditions; actual timelines may improve once the platform launches.

12. **PSE&G programs change frequently** — Verify all program details directly with PSE&G and NJ Clean Energy before relying on them for financial planning.

13. **SREC-II registration deadline** — Must register within 6 months of interconnection to lock in the 15-year ADI rate. Don't miss this deadline.

14. **Property tax exemption filing** — Must file Form CRES with the local tax assessor; not automatic. File as soon as installation is complete.

### How to Determine Your System Size

> **Step 1: Review your PSE&G bills**
> - Look at your last 12 months of usage (kWh) to determine annual consumption
> - Identify your average monthly cost and peak usage months
> - This determines the system size needed to offset your usage

> **Step 2: Assess your roof**
> - Check Google Maps satellite view for roof orientation, shading, and obstructions
> - South and southwest-facing roofs are optimal
> - Note any nearby trees, chimneys, or HVAC equipment that may shade panels

> **Step 3: Determine battery needs**
> - Estimate your evening peak load (4-9 PM usage)
> - A 13.5 kWh Powerwall can cover most of the evening for a typical home
> - Consider battery if you want backup power during outages or want to maximize TOU arbitrage

> **Step 4: Get quotes**
> - Contact 3-5 NJ-certified solar installers
> - Ask for detailed quotes with SREC-II income projections
> - Compare total cost, system size, and equipment options

> **Step 5: Check GSESP Phase 2**
> - Monitor njcleanenergy.com for residential battery incentive enrollment
> - If timing aligns with your installation, the battery rebate could significantly improve ROI
